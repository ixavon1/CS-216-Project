# Social Media, Policy & Engagement
**CS 216: Everything Data** | Spring 2026

McKenna Adams · Will Mayo · Ixa Sigler · Rhea Chelar

---

## Overview

This project investigates whether algorithmically mediated social media platforms privilege certain kinds of policy information, and whether that correlates with demographic factors like age, class, and immigration status.

---

## Research Questions

1. **Video Length & Detail** — Do platforms show shorter, less detailed policy content more than longer, more informative content, and does this differ across topics like immigration?
2. **Ad Spending & SES** — Does Facebook/Meta ad spending per capita differ by state and socioeconomic status?
3. **Immigration Framing** — Is immigration policy framed differently in high-immigrant vs. low-immigrant areas?
4. **Engagement vs. Quality** — Are engagement signals (likes, comments) negatively correlated with informational quality across policy topics?
